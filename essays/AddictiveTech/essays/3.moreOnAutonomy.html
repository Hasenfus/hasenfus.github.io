More on Autonomy: Children, AI, and Developmental Concerns

Building on our understanding of autonomy as self-determination, I want to explore how AI systems interact with human development, particularly in children and adolescents. This raises unique challenges that don't apply to fully autonomous adults.

The Developmental Framework

Normal humans are born with the potential capacity for self-determination, which matures through upbringing and exercise into a developed "full" capacity between infancy and adulthood. The "fullness" of this capacity is defined as a "range property"—sufficient capacity for self-determination. Agents with developed capacities have the full right to self-determination and are fit to be held fully responsible for their deeds.

If one has a lower capacity than that (due to age or disability), one has a right to take part in the autonomous governance of one's life. The capacities for self-determination are interpersonally and contextually malleable. The developmental stage of growing to be autonomous is clearly a special phase in life, where effects of AI or digitalized environments raise special concerns.

The Core Question

Based upon these ideas, and considering introducing access and communication of children with AI systems, is it paramount that a child should spend a considerable amount of their youth detached from such utilities? The consideration is born out of the idea of developed autonomy. However much, it is clear that the framing of 'good and developed' autonomy has certainly changed over the course of humanity.

Tool Use and Cognitive Evolution

The idea, as Stephen Wolfram noted—'over the course of history, those who used tools tended to do better off than those who didn't'. The introduction of tech in children's lives is inevitable, the question is how can we march forward with humans developing even stronger and more advanced cognitive, mental, and imaginative capacities with the offloading of cognitive tasks to an AI system.

You either use it or lose it, and if we are not going to be using it anymore, does it mean that there will exist a sharp decline or just a new normal baseline without such expertise? Almost like the drop in average typing speed because no one needed those anymore.

Insights Gleaned

Through this exploration, several insights have emerged: AI systems are no longer just tools—they're becoming developmental agents. They actively participate in shaping human capacities for self-determination, especially in children and adolescents. This realization changes everything about how we should design these systems.
